NYC Mobile Web

24 January 2012, 8 p.m. New York, NY

Come to the WeWork Lounge to see Matt Rogish provide an overview of Mulberry and how it fits into the PhoneGap ecosystem.

Boston Front End Developers Meetup

24 January 2012, 7 p.m. Boston, MA

In this talk, you’ll learn how you can use your HTML, CSS, and JavaScript chops to create great apps using Mulberry, without worrying about platform differences or the underlying plumbing that PhoneGap apps traditionally require.

PhoneGap Basic Training at thoughtbot Offices

27 January 2012, 9 a.m. — 4 p.m. Boston, MA

PhoneGap lets you take advantage of HTML, CSS, and JavaScript to create native mobile applications across multiple operating systems. If you’re interested in learning how you can bring your web development skills to a native environment, or if you’ve stumbled when trying to use PhoneGap in the past, this workshop is for you.

Details

HTML5 Mobile Apps Hackathon

28 January 2012, 9 a.m. — 6 p.m. Boston, MA

Spend the day hacking on HTML5 mobile apps at the amazing Bocoup Loft, and practice wielding your front-end fu in the mobile world. Come with your own idea, or find a fellow developer to team up with over breakfast. The event kicks off at 9 a.m., and the hacking starts in earnest at 10 a.m. At the end of the day, Toura will give an iPad 2 to the developer who makes the best app with Mulberry. We’ll have food and drink throughout the day, and when it’s all over, we’ll venture to a neighborhood bar to swap stories. Join us!
